Module 2 reflection

Module 2 was very informative, there was a lot of reading and information to absorb.

Knowing little about the history of the PC it was interesting to read about companies such as Microsoft, Apple and IBM who founded the industry, the people involved, Gates & Allen, Gary Kildall, Wozniak & Jobs, Bob Taylor, for example and the technology behind it, such as the microprocessor from Intel and various technologies from Xerox Parc.  It was also interesting to read the development from this point and how Moores law has underpinned this, continuing to do so.

In completing this module I have also learnt some practical aspects of computing, the basic architecture of a computer, the functions of hardware and software components, operating systems, binary bits and bytes, what effects computer power, the different types of computers and how they work, command-line and graphical user interfaces and the difference between analogue and digital.

Other practical tasks learnt which have helped with studying are note taking, reading skills, keeping an online study journal, and developing my web authoring skills further.

I can now give an informed account of the development of the pc, identifying the key people, organisations and technologies that played a part, now having the ability to explain the basic architecture of a computer and the functions of hardware & software, while understanding business concepts relevant to the PC industry. As such I have met with the objectives of the Module.
