Activity 2

Message sent to group conference from tutor introducing activity 2.

Hello everybody!

I hope you are all progressing nicely with the course.
This message contains the information needed to start the second tutor group activity. This may help you complete the first assignment.

What is this activity about?

The aim of the activity is to:

a) explore the principles behind netiquette and effective group working.
b) have a go at using the tutor group conference to reach a shared understanding within the group

The final outcome of this activity will be a list of ‘rules’ that we as a group can use to help make our collective work more effective and efficient over the rest of the year.

What do you need to do?

1. Review the material in Module 1, Section 2: Group working in cyberspace. This section lists articles that discuss issues about netiquette and group working.

2. From (at least one of) these articles note 1 or 2 ideas that you think our group could adopt to help us work together for the rest of the year.

3. Share your ideas with the rest of the group by posting a message about your ideas in this conference

4. Review what the rest of the group have suggested. Reply to comments where you agree or think an idea could be developed further.

5. Finally, review the whole group discussion and post a further message about any extra idea you have thought of as a result of the discussion.

When will the activity start and finish?

The activity starts from today and will end on the 6th March. This is 10 days before the assignment is due to give you time to finalise your work.

How will the list of principles for the group be drawn together?

I will summarise the discussion and create a list after the 6th of March. You will all need to check my list in case I miss anything. We can then review and develop this list as the year progresses.

A few tips:

Read the TMA before you prepare to take part in the activity, so that you know what you need to do. You may not need to read all the articles. Choose two that interest you. Then devise your first note for the conference. Once you have read other peoples ideas, you may want to read the other articles.

Email me if you feel that you need more clarification on any points.

I will give some more information in the following days.

Cheers,

Paolo
