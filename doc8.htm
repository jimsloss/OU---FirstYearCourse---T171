Module 3 reflection

I started this module with little knowledge about the internet despite using it at work for email and intranet purposes.  I have picked up a lot from this module, learning about the early visions of Vannevar Bush (Exercise 6) and JCR Licklider, to the development and introduction of the Arpanet by the D.O.D. in the 60s, its evolvment into the internet and of how the world wide web came to be part of this (6.3).  I obtained an understanding of the technology that makes it work, the protocols involved, the thinking of the people behind it, exploring the evolving culture on the net and how this has physically effected the internet itself (such as Usenet or the Open Source movement).

This module also allowed me to further develop skills introduced earlier, searching the web (Exercise 2) efficiently whilst assessing findings reliably, using an online diary, coping with information overload, and the creation of small web pages and sites.

I will continue to explore the technology mentioned in this module, as time and workload have not allowed me to investigate some aspects as fully as I would have liked to, however I feel that I have met with the objectives of the module, now having a clear understanding of exactly what the internet is (1.4), how it came to be, the technology that makes it work, the people and companies/organisations that made it possible, and the culture of the net in todays world.
