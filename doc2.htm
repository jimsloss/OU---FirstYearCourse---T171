Group working

This course has been my first attempt at working within a group on-line and I have come to few conclusions regarding my experience.

Group working on-line certainly proved to have its advantages;

  • The discomfort or biased views that might normally present themselves in group meetings on race, colour, religion or gender are absent.
  • Each person can contribute whenever their own schedule allowed it and from wherever was most suitable to them.
  • Group members tend to discuss problems they have come across and how they were resolved.
  • The 'project' is an ongoing discussion, with continuing contribution.
  • Group activity on-line can be more productive, ideas can be discussed more openly and investigated more thoroughly.
  • There is more time to think and review participation before contributing.
  • Feedback is more creative.
  • There is a reviewable record of the discussion to date.
Some dis-advantages also emerged;
  • There can be cluttered, unclear or irrelevant messages.
  • Some group members can be unwilling or unable to partake due to lack of ability.
  • Meeting deadlines or keeping to agreed timetables can be harder, as all members work at their own speed.
  • Body-language is missing, therefore its easier to take things out-of-context or mis-interpret them. There is need for greater care in what you are saying, and how you say it.
