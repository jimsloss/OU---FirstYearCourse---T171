Reading and taking notes

At various points in the course, you will be asked to read documents, take notes and make summaries of them. To be able to do so effectively is a skill, to be learnt like any other.

The key resource for this activity is Chapter 2 of the book, The Sciences Good Study Guide, by Northedge, Thomas, Lane and Peasgood. If it appeals to you, you may wish to buy the book. It is available in, or can be ordered from, most book shops.

The chapter is presented in a form which looks exactly like the printed book.

One major advantage of presenting the chapter in this form is that you don't have to be online to read it. Be sure to disconnect once it has been downloaded. You then have the choice of reading it on your screen or printing it out and reading it on paper.

If you read it on your screen you will have to learn the basics of Acrobat Reader, which is not difficult. The main point is to adjust the size of the image so that it is comfortable for you to read. I find it is easiest if I select the Acrobat's View menu and set it to 'Fit width', 'Continuous' and 'Thumbnails and Page'. Then all you have to do is scroll to read the whole chapter.

Read The Sciences Good Study Guide, Chapter 2 - Reading and making notes (link not operational, but when you clicked on this it downloaded to your hard disk, and opened automatically using 'Acrobat Reader'.). You will also need the 'Collee paper', which is used as an example for note taking.

This chapter was written assuming you would be using paper for your notes, but principles in it apply to all forms of note taking. You may choose to make your notes on paper, or alternatively, you could make them in your word processor and save them in your 'Module 1 notes' document.
