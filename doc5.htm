The learning to learn principle
(tma02 question and response, proof of learning)

(ii) Explain the key principles or techniques you have found which help in learning about computers. This should demonstrate your 'learning to learn' skills. It should explain how, other than being shown specific commands and key-presses, you can increase your computing abilities.

(ii)
Computers can be difficult and temperamental at the best of times but when you are new to computers  the difficulty is increased tenfold.  There are so many different computers and computer applications on the market that learning to use them can prove quite a challenge, but there are steps you can take to minimise your efforts and maximise your ability.

I would consider myself to be familiar with computers, but the course I am undertaking has taught me  something that I had taken for granted and not realised the importance of.  When you are new to a computer or application the best thing to do is to explore it.  Explore the menus, the functions, the help screens, the filing system, the desktop, and don’t be afraid to try things out, you cannot damage your computer in this way.  Learn from mistakes you make, look for workarounds, there’s always more than one way to do something, and in most cases keyboard shortcuts can be more effective than the standard method.

Not only does this method prove most effective, but it is also an extremely fast way of rewarding yourself for your efforts and gaining quick results.  The more you explore the more familiar you get with the way it works, and the more you get out of it.
