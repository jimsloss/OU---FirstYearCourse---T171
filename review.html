Review of: “Netiquette, or the social conventions of computer conferencing”


Key Points for Group working

Come to a shared understanding or consensus.

  • Try to understand other peoples views
  • Find conclusion which satisfies everyone
Allowing groups to engage with issues, resolving disagreements without upsets and hurt feelings.
  • Thank, acknowledge and support people freely
  • Acknowledge before differing
  • Speak from your own perspective, present other’s views with quotes / acknowledgement
  • Try to avoid ‘absolute’ statements.
  • Avoid ‘flaming’ spirals    -    use emoticons      -    be careful with use of capitals
Use Internet ‘conventions’ carefully
  • Quoting is best used sparingly
  • Check messages are addressed to the most appropriate place
  • Avoid irrelevant subject matter
  • Avoid multiple answers to any questions.
  • Keep messages short, writing concisely helps with the flow of discussion
  • Ensure subject line clear, indicating content clearly
  • Bear in mind copyright is also applicable on-line
In general, be careful, use common sense at all times.
